Dr. Jose Rizal

National Hero of the Philippines

Early Life and Education

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was born on June 19, 1861, in Calamba, Laguna. A polymath, nationalist, and ophthalmologist, he was a key figure in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. He excelled in various fields including medicine, poetry, essay-writing, and linguistics, fluent in at least 22 languages.

  • Born: June 19, 1861, Calamba, Laguna
  • Parents: Francisco Mercado Rizal and Teodora Alonso Realonda
  • Early Education: Biñan, Laguna
  • Higher Education: Ateneo Municipal de Manila, University of Santo Tomas, Universidad Central de Madrid

Literary Works and Activism

Rizal's most influential works, "Noli Me Tángere" (Touch Me Not) and "El filibusterismo" (The Reign of Greed), exposed the corruption and abuses of the Spanish friars and colonial government. These novels ignited the flames of revolution among Filipinos.

Noli Me Tángere (1887)

A passionate exposé of the injustices under Spanish colonial rule, criticizing the Catholic friars and the Spanish government.

El filibusterismo (1891)

A darker, more revolutionary novel, serving as a sequel to Noli Me Tángere, depicting the Philippines on the brink of revolution.

Martyrdom and Legacy

Rizal's activism led to his arrest and trial for rebellion, sedition, and conspiracy. Despite his denial of direct involvement in violent revolution, he was found guilty and sentenced to death. He was executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896, in Bagumbayan (now Rizal Park), Manila, at the age of 35.

His death, however, did not quell the revolution; instead, it fueled it further, making him a martyr and an enduring symbol of Filipino nationalism. His ideals continue to inspire generations of Filipinos.
