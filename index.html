About Jose Rizal

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (June 19, 1861 – December 30, 1896) was a Filipino nationalist, polymath, and revolutionary during the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines. He is widely considered one of the greatest heroes of the Philippines.

Early Life and Education

Born in Calamba, Laguna, Rizal showed intellectual prowess from a young age. He studied in Ateneo Municipal de Manila and later pursued ophthalmology in Spain, where he became a prominent figure in the Propaganda Movement.

Literary Works and Activism

Rizal's novels, "Noli Me Tángere" (Touch Me Not) and "El filibusterismo" (The Reign of Greed), exposed the injustices of Spanish colonial rule and ignited the flames of Philippine nationalism. He also wrote numerous essays, poems, and articles advocating for reforms.

Martyrdom and Legacy

Despite advocating for peaceful reforms, Rizal was accused of rebellion and executed by firing squad in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park) at the age of 35. His death solidified his status as a martyr and inspired the Philippine Revolution.
